Introduction

Background

The Swiss Confederation was founded in 1291 as a defensive alliance among three cantons. In succeeding years, other localities joined the original three. The Swiss Confederation secured its independence from the Holy Roman Empire in 1499.

A constitution of 1848, subsequently modified in 1874 to allow voters to introduce referenda on proposed laws, replaced the confederation with a centralized federal government. Switzerland's sovereignty and neutrality have long been honored by the major European powers, and the country was not involved in either of the two World Wars.

The political and economic integration of Europe over the past half century, as well as Switzerland's role in many UN and international organizations, has strengthened Switzerland's ties with its neighbors. However, the country did not officially become a UN member until 2002. Switzerland remains active in many UN and international organizations but retains a strong commitment to neutrality.

Geography

Location

Switzerland is locate in Central Europe, just east of France and north of Italy. It's coordinates are 47.00 N, 8.00 E.

Climate

The climate of Switzerland can be described as temperate, although that varies with altitude. Winters are cold, cloud, rainy, and snowy, while summers are humid with occasional showers.

Government

Citizenship

Switzerland does not grant citizenship soley by birth. At least one parent must already be a citizen of Switzerland.

Dual citizenship is recognized in Switzerland. In order to achieve naturalization, you must be a resident of Switzerland for 12 years, including at least 3 of the last 5 years prior to application.

Government Type

Switzerland has a federal republic government system. It was formally a confederation.

National Anthem

The Swiss anthem has four names:

  • "Schweizerpsalm" (German)
  • "Cantique Suisse" (French)
  • "Salmo svizzero" (Italian)
  • "Psalm svizzer" (Romansch)

The Swiss anthem was unofficially adopted in 1961, then officially adopted in 1981. The anthem has been popular in a number of swiss cantons since it's composition (in German) in 1841.

It was translated into the other three official languages of the country, and it is official in each of those languages.
